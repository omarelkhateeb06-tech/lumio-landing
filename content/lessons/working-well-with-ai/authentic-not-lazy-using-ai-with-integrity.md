## reading

A fair worry sits under a lot of AI use: am I being smart, or am I being lazy? Is this still my work? The honest answer is that it depends entirely on how you use the tool, and the difference is worth naming clearly so you can stay on the right side of it without guilt.

The line is this: **AI with integrity amplifies your thinking. Lazy AI replaces it.**

When you bring the substance, the judgment, and the decisions, and use AI to draft faster, organize better, or get unstuck, you are the author and the tool is a tool. That is no more cheating than using a calculator or a spell-checker. When you hand over the thinking entirely, paste the output unread, and present it as considered work you never actually considered, you have crossed into something else, and people can usually tell.

Here is what staying authentic looks like in practice.

**You direct, it drafts.** The ideas, angle, and key points come from you. The AI helps you express them. A first draft you shaped is yours. A generic answer you requested and rubber-stamped is not.

**You edit it into your voice.** Raw AI output has a recognizable flatness. When you revise it so it sounds like you and says what you actually mean, you are reclaiming authorship. The editing is where the work becomes yours again.

**You can stand behind every word.** The real test: could you defend this if asked? Do you understand it, agree with it, and know it is true? If you would be caught out the moment someone questioned a line, you outsourced too much.

**You are honest when it matters.** In contexts where original or personal work is expected, you are transparent about the tool's role rather than hiding it.

Using AI is not the lazy choice. Using it to avoid thinking is. The same tool can make you sharper or hollow you out, and which one happens is decided entirely by whether you stay in the author's chair.
