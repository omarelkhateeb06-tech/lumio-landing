## reading

Here is an unfair situation that is becoming common: you write something yourself, run it through nobody, submit it, and get told a detector flagged it as AI-generated. A teacher, an editor, or a client is now questioning your honesty over a tool that, frankly, does not work very well.

Understand the core fact first: **AI detectors are not reliable.** They do not detect AI in any real sense. They guess, based on how "predictable" the writing looks, and clear, competent, well-structured human writing often looks predictable. Non-native English speakers, people who write plainly, and anyone who edits carefully get falsely flagged regularly. A detector result is an opinion, not evidence.

That said, being right does not automatically protect you. Here is how to handle it.

**Keep your process, not just your product.** The single best protection is evidence that you did the work. Draft in a tool that saves version history, keep your notes and outlines, and do not delete the messy early versions. A visible trail of drafts is almost impossible to fake and settles the question fast.

**If you are accused, stay calm and lead with proof.** Defensiveness reads as guilt. Instead: "I understand the detector flagged this, and I know those tools have high error rates. Here's my draft history and my research notes so you can see how it came together. I'm happy to walk you through my thinking." Calm plus evidence beats outrage every time.

**Educate, gently.** Many people do not know detectors are unreliable. Sharing that these tools routinely flag human writing, ideally with a link to a reputable source, reframes the conversation from "did you cheat" to "is this tool trustworthy," which is the real question.

**Push for a better standard.** Where you can, encourage relying on process and conversation over detector scores. "Ask me to explain any part of it" is a fairer test than a number from a tool that admits its own error rate.

You cannot control a flawed detector. You can control whether you have a paper trail and whether you respond like someone with nothing to hide, because you do not.
