## reading

The last lesson said paste the real material, because that's where good answers come from. This lesson is the guardrail on that advice. Pasting real text is powerful precisely because it's real, and "real" sometimes means sensitive. A quick habit keeps the power without the risk.

**Assume what you paste may be stored and reviewed.** Depending on the tool and its settings, your inputs can be retained, used to improve models, or seen by staff during reviews. Treat the chat box like a postcard, not a sealed vault. That doesn't mean never paste anything; it means paste with a moment's thought about what you're sending.

**The five-second check, before you hit enter.** Ask yourself three quick questions about the text:
- **Could it identify a real person?** Names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, account numbers, medical or HR details. Personal data deserves extra caution.
- **Is it confidential or under an agreement?** Unreleased financials, client material under NDA, proprietary code, anything stamped internal or privileged.
- **Would it hurt someone or the business if it leaked?** Passwords, security details, private complaints, anything embarrassing or damaging out of context.

If all three are clearly "no," paste freely. If any is "yes," don't just paste and hope.

**When the answer is "yes," you have three good moves:**
1. **Strip and anonymize.** Replace names with "the client," numbers with "[amount]," specifics with placeholders. The AI helps you draft the reply just as well, and nothing identifying ever leaves your hands.
2. **Use the approved tool.** Many workplaces provide an enterprise AI with a contract that data won't be used for training. If yours does, sensitive-but-permitted work belongs there, not in a personal free account.
3. **Don't paste it.** Some things, passwords, certain personal or legal records, simply shouldn't go in. Do that part yourself and use AI for the rest.

**The mindset, not a rulebook:** you don't need to memorize regulations. You need one reflex: a half-second pause before pasting anything that involves a real person or a real secret. That pause is the entire skill. Build it now, while the stakes are low, and it protects you automatically once the work gets real.

## mini_project

Your 3-minute exercise: think of a real document you'd genuinely want AI's help with this week, then run the five-second check on it. Could it identify someone? Is it confidential? Would a leak cause harm? Decide your move: paste as-is, strip-and-paste, or don't paste. Doing this once on real material turns the check into a reflex.
