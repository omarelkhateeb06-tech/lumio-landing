## reading

You've opened the tool. There's a blinking cursor and a box that says "Ask anything." This is the moment most people type "hello," get a polite reply, and quietly close the tab. Let's not do that. Here's how to spend the next five minutes so you walk away with a real win.

**Don't test it. Use it.** The instinct is to quiz the AI with a trivia question or "tell me a joke." That teaches you nothing about how it helps *you*. Instead, grab something small and real from your actual day: an email you've been putting off, a paragraph that reads badly, a decision you're chewing on, a list you need to organize. Real work is where the value lives.

**Give it the situation, not just a command.** Compare "write an email" with "I need to tell my team our Friday meeting is moved to Monday at 10am, keep it short and friendly." The second one has context, and the answer will be ten times better. You don't need perfect phrasing. You just need to say what you'd tell a helpful colleague who's stepping in cold.

**Read the answer like a draft, not a verdict.** The first response is a starting point. If it's too long, say "make it shorter." If the tone is off, say "more casual." You are allowed to talk back, and the answer improves every time you do. (There's a whole lesson on this loop coming up.)

**Then actually use it.** Send the email. Paste the cleaned-up paragraph. The win isn't a clever conversation with a robot; it's a real task finished faster than usual. That's the feeling that turns AI from a curiosity into a habit.

Five minutes, one real task, one finished result. That's the entire goal of your first session.
