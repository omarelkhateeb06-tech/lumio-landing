## reading

Every time you open a new ChatGPT conversation, you're starting from zero. The model doesn't know you're a product manager, doesn't know you prefer bullet points, doesn't know you're writing for a UK audience. That's why every first message has to re-establish context.

**Custom instructions** (in ChatGPT) and **system prompts** (in Claude/API) solve this. They're persistent context injected before every conversation, essentially a permanent brief.

**What to put in your custom instructions:**

*Who you are:* "I'm a product manager at a Series B SaaS startup. My team is 8 people. My manager is non-technical."

*How you want responses:* "Be direct. Skip preamble. Use bullet points for lists. If you're uncertain, say so explicitly."

*Tone and style:* "I write in a conversational but professional tone. No buzzwords. No em-dashes used for emphasis."

*Recurring context:* Anything you find yourself re-explaining frequently, such as your company's product, your audience, your constraints.

**What not to put in:** Task-specific instructions. Custom instructions are for persistent identity, not one-off tasks.

Setting this up takes 15 minutes once. It makes every subsequent conversation 30% better without any extra effort on your part.

## mini_project

Your 5-minute exercise: Open ChatGPT settings → 'Customize ChatGPT' (or Claude's profile settings). Write your custom instructions using the template above: who you are, how you want responses, your tone, and one piece of recurring context.
