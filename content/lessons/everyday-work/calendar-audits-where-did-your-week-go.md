## reading

Most people estimate their time spent on different activities very inaccurately. They feel busy, they feel productive, but when they audit their calendar they find that meetings they thought took 30% of their week actually took 55%.

**The audit:** Export or screenshot your last week's calendar. Paste the rough schedule into ChatGPT with this prompt:

*"Here's my calendar for last week: [paste]. Please: 1) Categorize each block into: Meetings, Deep work, Admin, External calls, Unclear. 2) Calculate the approximate percentage of working time in each category. 3) Flag any patterns that might indicate time leaks. 4) Suggest three specific changes to next week's calendar."*

The output gives you a clear breakdown in 30 seconds that would take 20 minutes to do manually.

**What to do with it:** You're not trying to optimize every minute. You're trying to see if your calendar reflects your actual priorities. If "deep work on the roadmap" is 80% of your stated priority but 15% of your actual calendar, that gap is the problem.

Run this audit once a month. It takes 5 minutes and the patterns it reveals tend to be uncomfortable but actionable.

## mini_project

Your 5-minute exercise: Do the calendar audit for last week right now. Paste your schedule (rough is fine) and run the prompt above. What category surprised you most?
