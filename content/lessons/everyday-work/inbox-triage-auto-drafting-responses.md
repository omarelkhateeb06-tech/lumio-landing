## reading

Most professional emails follow a handful of patterns: updates, requests, declines, follow-ups, introductions. Once you recognize the pattern, drafting becomes mostly structural, and structure is exactly what AI is good at.

**The triage system:**

**Tier 1: Auto-draft:** Routine emails (status updates, meeting confirmations, standard follow-ups). Paste the incoming email + your 2-3 bullet points of what you want to say. Prompt: *"Draft a professional reply to this email. My key points: [bullets]. Tone: [warm/direct/formal]. Max 150 words."*

**Tier 2: Draft + edit:** Sensitive or relationship-important emails. Let the AI draft, but you edit substantively. Use it to get past the blank page, not to sign off without reading.

**Tier 3: Write yourself:** Anything where the nuance is everything, a difficult conversation, a negotiation, something that will be forwarded or quoted.

The mistake people make is applying Tier 3 effort to Tier 1 emails. A meeting confirmation doesn't need your full creative attention.

**The voice trick:** For Tier 1 emails, voice-dictate your thoughts in 30 seconds. That dictation becomes your brief.

## mini_project

Your 5-minute exercise: Find 3 emails in your drafts or inbox that you've been avoiding. Classify each as Tier 1, 2, or 3. Draft the Tier 1 ones using the auto-draft method above. Send at least one.
