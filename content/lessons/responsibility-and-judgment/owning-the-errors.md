## reading

Here is a principle that will save your reputation: when work goes out under your name, you are accountable for it, no matter what helped you make it. "The AI got it wrong" is true and completely irrelevant. You chose the tool, you reviewed the output, and you sent it. The accountability stays with the human, exactly as it would if an intern drafted something and you signed off without reading it.

This sounds harsh, but it is actually freeing, because it puts the one thing you control front and center: your final check before the work leaves your hands.

**The check is the job.** Using AI does not remove your responsibility, it relocates it. Your value moves from writing every word to verifying and deciding. The minute you treat the review as optional, you have accepted the AI's mistakes as your own without even seeing them. Skipping the check is the actual error; the hallucination is just what reveals it.

**If something slips through, own it cleanly.** Despite good habits, an AI-assisted mistake will eventually reach someone. When it does, the move that protects you is not blaming the tool. It is the same as any professional error: acknowledge it, fix it, say what you will do differently. "I missed that in my review, here's the correction" rebuilds trust. "The AI made it up" sounds like you do not understand that you were supposed to catch it.

**Never let the tool be the scapegoat.** Reaching for "the AI did it" signals that you will do it again, because you have not accepted the part that was yours. People forgive a caught-and-owned mistake quickly. They do not forgive someone who blames their tools.

**Adjust the system, not just the apology.** A real error owns up to the gap that let it through and closes it: a tighter check on numbers, a second read on client work, a rule about what never goes out unverified. That is what turns a mistake into a stronger process.

The freeing part is this: you cannot control whether AI occasionally invents something, but you fully control whether you check before you send and how you respond if something gets through. Own both, and AI makes you faster without ever making you less trustworthy.
