## reading

Most people treat ChatGPT and Google as interchangeable. They're not. Getting this wrong costs you 20 minutes of bad results before you give up and switch anyway.

**Google is for facts with a source you can verify.** Stock prices, news, what a regulation actually says. Google indexes the real world in near-real-time. ChatGPT doesn't. Its knowledge has a cutoff date and it can't check URLs.

**ChatGPT is for tasks that require reasoning, synthesis, or generation.** Drafting an email. Explaining a concept in simpler terms. Restructuring a document. Brainstorming five angles on a problem. Anywhere you'd otherwise stare at a blank page.

The 10-second test: ask yourself "does this question have one correct answer that exists somewhere in the world?" If yes, Google. If the answer needs to be *created* or *reasoned through*, ChatGPT.

Where people go wrong: they ask ChatGPT for facts ("what's the current interest rate?") and get confident-sounding nonsense. Or they Google "how should I structure my performance review?" and get 14 generic listicles instead of something tailored to their situation.

There's a third category: **research synthesis**, which is where you use both. Google to find the primary sources. ChatGPT to help you understand, compare, and summarize them. That combination is genuinely powerful and most people never do it.

## mini_project

Your 5-minute exercise: Write down the last 5 things you Googled in the past two days. For each one, ask: was this a fact-lookup or a reasoning/creation task? How many could have been better served by ChatGPT? Pick one and try it now.
