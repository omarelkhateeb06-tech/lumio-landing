## reading

Competitive intelligence is one of those things everyone agrees is important and almost no one does consistently. The problem is collection: someone has to go check all the sources on some cadence, and that person usually stops after two weeks.

**The monitoring stack:**

**Tier 1: Free, automated:**
- Google Alerts for competitor names + key terms
- LinkedIn notifications on competitor company pages
- RSS feeds for their blog (Feedly, free tier)

**Tier 2: Weekly synthesis:**
Once per week, dump any alerts/posts/news into ChatGPT: *"Here are updates from my competitors this week: [paste]. Summarize: 1) Any product changes, 2) Any positioning shifts, 3) Any strategy signals, 4) One implication for us."*

**Tier 3: Quarterly deep dive:**
Every quarter, give ChatGPT each competitor's current homepage copy, pricing page, and job postings: *"Compare these competitors across: positioning, target customer, price point, growth signals. What gaps are they leaving that we could own?"*

The stack requires setup once and 20 minutes weekly. Most teams track competitors 0 hours per week until they're surprised.

## mini_project

Your 5-minute exercise: Set up Google Alerts for your two main competitors right now. While you're there, write one sentence describing each competitor's current positioning.
