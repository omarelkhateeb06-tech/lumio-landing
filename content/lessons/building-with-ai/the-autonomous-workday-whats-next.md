## reading

We've spent 29 lessons building AI fluency: using models to draft, synthesize, research, analyze, and create. That's the first era of working with AI. The second era, already beginning for early adopters, is autonomous agents: systems that operate continuously, make decisions, and take actions without requiring your input for every step.

**What's already possible:**
- Email triage agents that prioritize, draft, and send routine responses based on rules you define
- Research agents that monitor sources and deliver briefings without being asked
- Content pipelines that generate, review, and schedule without a human in the loop
- Customer service agents that handle tier-1 issues and escalate only edge cases

**What the threshold moment looks like for you:** It's the day you realize you spent an hour on work that ran automatically in the background. And the quality was good enough that you approved it without changes.

**How to prepare:**
1. **Document your decision rules.** The processes you've made explicit are the candidates for automation. Agents can follow rules; they can't infer undocumented preferences.
2. **Build tolerance for imperfect outputs.** The comparison isn't "agent vs. perfection": it's "agent + your review vs. you doing it manually."
3. **Start small and high-frequency.** Automate recurring low-stakes tasks first.

The 5 minutes you've invested each day for 30 days isn't about individual productivity tricks. It's about building the mental model to navigate a decade of change.
